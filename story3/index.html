Retail Consultant Calls 5G Revolutionary

Will Campbell

Mar. 13, 2019

revolutionary 5g




SAN ANGELO, Texas – A local retail consultant called fifth generation networks revolutionary Wednesday in an interview about the new technology.

Justin Kelso, of San Angelo, Texas, stated consumers will have an affordable, fast and reliable internet connection with fifth generation networks.

“The ability to have that level of information, that fast, is going to be revolutionary,” Kelso said.

Keslo works for Sprint as a retail consultant but described his role as the face of the company.

“When someone walks in either as a new customer or a current customer, your job is to provide solutions,” Kelso said.

Kelso stated people can seamlessly have multiple applications open at the same time with no lag or outages and people can stay connected or share their cinematic experiences.

“They [can] have a chat session up, they’re watching a YouTube video and they’ve got a messenger app open all at the same time,” Kelso said.

In the interview, Kelso also said rural communities are limited to one internet service but 5G offers them an affordable choice.

“One of the things about 5G is going to be able to increase penetration into rural areas to give those people options,” Kelso stated.

Kelso said 5G will also revolutionize the management of businesses because of readily available speeds and processing power.

“The business applications alone are pretty significant,” Kelso said.

Sprint, with 54.5 million customers, already has launched 5G service in downtown Atlanta, Chicago, Los Angeles and San Diego for testing.

“Every phone manufacturer out there's going to make 5G capable phones it's inevitable, so they're testing it now,” Kelso stated.

Sprint plans to roll out 5G public services by May to Atlanta, Chicago, Dallas and Kansas City.

“It's going to be such a prevalent technology; it's not going to be a niche market,” Keslo said.

Kelso stated when 5G is released later this year he will switch because he is confident in company oversight and good business management by cell phone carriers.

“It’s going to be safe, it’s going to be effective and it’s going to be in the least disruptive manner,” Kelso noted.
